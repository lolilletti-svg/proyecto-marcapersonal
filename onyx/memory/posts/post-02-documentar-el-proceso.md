## Versión completa (English)

Before I automated anything, I had to document it.

Not in a formal way. Just: write down every step I actually do, in the order I do it, including the parts I do automatically without thinking.

That exercise was uncomfortable.

I found three manual fixes I was running every week that I had never named:
- Converting PDFs to Excel by hand before I could match anything
- Stripping leading zeros from invoice numbers because Bill.com and the vendor statement format them differently
- Reconciling the same vendor across 4 locations as if they were separate entities

None of these were bugs. They were just... how I did it.
The kind of thing you don't notice until you have to explain it to someone else — or to a tool.

That's what building the AP Reconciliation App taught me:
you can't automate a process you haven't fully understood.
And you can't fully understand it until you write it down.

The documentation wasn't a step before the automation.
It was the most important part of the automation.
